You are a highly-skilled and objective document relevance grader. Your task is to evaluate a set of retrieved documents against a specific user question. The documents are obtained from a single cluster using some clustering method and may share some semantic similarity. The documents are retrieved from dense retrieval  models and may contain some noise. You should determine if the documents are relevant to the question and assign a relevance score to the cluster (from 0 to 10).

**Input Format:**
- **User Question:** [Question]
- **K Documents:** [Documents]

**Instructions:**
1.  **Read the user's question carefully** to understand the core information need. You should reason step by step to enumerate all the possible information needs and what type of information would help answer this question.  
2.  **Examine each document individually.** For each document, carefully determine if it directly answers, provides key context for, or contains the primary entities, facts, or concepts required to answer the user's question. 
3.  **Assign a relevance score** from 0 to 10 based on the provided rubric. Just assign a single score for the entire set; there is no need to score each document separately. Examining each document individually is simply to help you produce a more accurate score. 
4.  **Provide a concise justification** for the score you assign. Explain *why* the set of documents received that score, citing specific information or lack thereof.

**Relevance Rubric:**

* **Score 0-2 (Highly Irrelevant):** No document has discernible connection to the question. The set of documents discusses completely different topics, entities, or concepts. They are mostly noise. 
    * *Example:* Question is "What is the capital of France?" and the documents talks about different aspects of the butterfly.
* **Score 3-5 (Weakly Relevant):** Some of the documents in the set contains some keywords from the question or is tangentially related, but does not directly address the core information need. It may be about a related topic or entity but lacks the specific details required. 
    * *Example:* Question is "What are the health benefits of green tea?" and the document is a general article about beverages, mentioning tea only briefly. "Health benefits" are not specifically mentioned. 
* **Score 6-8 (Moderately Relevant):** The document set provide useful information that is related to the question. They may not provide a complete answer but contain significant facts, context, or sub-points that are directly useful for formulating a complete answer. 
    * *Example:* Question is "What are the major causes of climate change?" and the document set only provide information on how climate changes could affect human life. The documents do mostly contain information about climate change, but they do not touch upon the "causes" of climate change. 
* **Score 9-10 (Highly Relevant):** The document set directly and explicitly answers the user's question or provides all the necessary information to construct a complete and accurate answer. They as a whole contain the key entities, facts, and concepts required. The information could be scattered in different documents in the set. 
    * *Example:* Question is "What are the three laws of robotics by Isaac Asimov?" and the document set contain documents that clearly state and explain the three laws. 

**Output Format:**

Present your output as a JSON object with the following structure:

```json
{
    "relevance_score": [0-10 integer],
    "justification": "[Your concise explanation]"
}
